② Cross-brand benchmark — Topics are universal, Plugs are brand-specific

When a customer reports the same food quality issue at Behrouz, OvenStory and Wendy's — the Topic system calls it Food Quality everywhere. The Plug system gives it a different name depending on which brand they ordered from — making cross-brand comparison impossible.

④ Actionability — Sub-topics tell you exactly what to fix, Plugs leave you guessing

Every sub-topic maps to a specific operational action. The Plug "Taste" covers 10,025 reviews — but which taste issue? Undercooked? Cold? Stale? Foreign object? Without a sub-topic you can't act.

✓ Sub-topic → Clear action
✗ Plug "Taste" = no actionable information
⑤ Brand-context dependency — Topics work anywhere, Plugs need brand lookup

Classify a review without knowing the brand. The Topic system still gives you a clean answer. The Plug system cannot — it uses a different taxonomy for OvenStory vs Behrouz vs Faasos vs Wendy's.

Bottom line
Topic / Sub-topic wins on every dimension that matters for analytics: consistency, comparability, actionability, and clarity. The Plug system was designed for kitchen operations — routing issues to the right team within a brand. It was never designed for cross-brand benchmarking or trend analysis, and it shows.

Use Topics for the dashboard and all management reporting. Plugs remain useful for kitchen-level SOPs and daily QC logs within individual brands — but the moment you want to compare Behrouz against Faasos against OvenStory on equal footing, only the Topic system can do that.
